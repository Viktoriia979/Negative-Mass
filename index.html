Negative Mass:

Negative mass is a theoretical form of matter that would have a mass less than zero. Unlike ordinary matter, which is attracted by gravity, negative mass would behave in very unusual ways. For example, if you pushed an object with negative mass, it would accelerate toward you instead of away from you. Scientists have never observed negative mass in nature, and it remains a hypothetical concept in physics.

Negative mass is important because it appears in some theoretical models that attempt to explain mysteries in the universe. Some scientists have suggested that it could help explain dark energy, the mysterious force causing the expansion of the universe to accelerate. It has also been proposed as a possible ingredient for keeping wormholes open or enabling advanced concepts such as warp drives, although these ideas remain highly speculative.

If negative mass exists, it could have strange interactions with ordinary matter. For example, a positive-mass object and a negative-mass object placed together could theoretically accelerate endlessly in the same direction, a phenomenon sometimes called "runaway motion." Because negative mass has never been detected, scientists do not know whether it is physically possible or simply a mathematical curiosity. Research into negative mass continues because it may provide insights into gravity, cosmology, and the fundamental laws of physics.
